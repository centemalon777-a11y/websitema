Scalar and Vector Quantity

Scalar Quantity

A scalar quantity is a physical quantity that has magnitude only and no direction.
Examples: Mass, Temperature, Speed, Distance, Energy, Time.

Vector Quantity

A vector quantity is a physical quantity that has both magnitude and direction.
Examples: Displacement, Velocity, Acceleration, Force, Momentum, Weight.

Key Differences

  • Scalars are described completely by a number and a unit (e.g., 5 kg, 20Â°C).
  • Vectors require both a number (magnitude), a unit, and a direction (e.g., 10 m/s north, 5 N downward).
